About Calvin Yoon

I am a data scientist with a passion for advancing healthcare. I have a Masters in Integrative Bioscience and Biotechology (graduated Summa Cum Laude / Highest Honors) from POSTECH in South Korea and a Bachelors in Biomedical Engineering with a concentration of Cell & Tissue Engineering from the University of Rochester. In academia, my research was focused on developing imaging methods for characterizing the tissue microenvironment of cancer and inflammation. Industry Projects

Project 1 - MFG Automation

To reduce the cost of femoral implant to the patient, Fortune 500 company (F500) acquires a small Research & Development company with proprietary metal-injection moulding technology and aspired to build a fully automated manufacturing process that can meet production and product quality requirements. This would be F5 America’s first fully automated manufacturing cell for this class of product. Calvin led a cross-functional team of 20 (engineering, IT, security, marketing, legal, quality, and vendors) to conceptualize and build this process. He drafted and executed requirements testing via statement of work, user requirement specification, and validations. He wrote requirements of machine vision with Fanuc, to reduce false product processing. He implemented innovative manufacturing processes that saved $3M in development costs for this $40M project and improved a 3D measurement analysis technique that reduced engineering analysis time by 15 hours a week, or 90%. His team won the 2020 F500 Collaboration Award for overcoming logistical challenges brought by COVID-19 and meeting original deadlines. For improving the efficiency of business operations, F500 aimed to standardize its method of acquiring production data and eliminate data silos. Calvin, as a representative of the F5 site, designed a roadmap and led team of 5 to implement the site’s first Internet of Things (IoT) data acquisition system to automatically track production Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) per ISA-95. He and his team implement IoT for 1200+ machines using Ignition, Azure, and Power Bi for acquisition, analysis, and visualization of production KPIs.
